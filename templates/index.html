Adventures

In the past, I've:

  • Scaled scientific moonshots at Schmidt Futures (funding frontier research and building high-risk science programs, Policy Entrepreneurs Network, and philanthropic funds).
  • Built FacePrint, emotion AI for early detection of neurodegenerative disorders. Moved to SF at 18 to spin it out. Startup failed—best education.
  • Conducted computational neuroscience research at Stanford, UCSF, MIT, Harvard, Weizmann Institute and apprenticed in dementia and movement disorder clinics.
  • Cultivated communities of founders and technologists as Interact Fellowship Retreat Director and policy entrepreneurs as PEN co-founder.
  • Given talks at MIT Media Lab, WIRED Health, Smithsonian, United Nations General Assembly Science Summit, European Patent Office, countless schools and more.
  • Studied Symbolic Systems at Stanford (computer science, philosophy, linguistics, neuroscience, psychology).
  • Started in my first official lab at ten after years experimenting in my childhood kitchen lab (beginning with the biochemistry of yogurt).
  • Grew up middle child of five in Kansas, raised by a single mom—learned to be scrappy and root for underdogs.

Whether through Mahler, brain imaging, or delightful user experiences, I'm drawn to ideas and people that spark awe.

I respond to cold emails and believe in people early. I love nerding out with people who pair ambition with earnestness, imagination with pragmatism, and curiosity with kindness.

I've met some of my favorite people through Interact, Thiel Fellowship, YC, and RSI. I believe the best is still ahead.
